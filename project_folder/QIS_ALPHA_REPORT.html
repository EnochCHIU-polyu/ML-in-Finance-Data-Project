Executive Thesis.

This report summarizes a production-oriented machine-learning alpha framework that combines cluster-aware equity modelling with sentiment-driven residual correction and multi-horizon signal governance. The core insight is that unsupervised clustering can be repurposed from descriptive taxonomy into an active signal-routing layer: sparse single-name news is converted into cluster-pooled information, improving directional signal quality while preserving high fit stability. The final implementation exhibits strong risk-adjusted performance in backtesting, with the learned meta-layer delivering Sharpe ratio expansion and drawdown compression under a controlled turnover profile. Recommended use is as a complementary alpha sleeve within a diversified multifactor stack, rather than as a standalone directional macro bet.

1. Executive Summary

We propose an ML-driven alpha signal designed for integration into an institutional multifactor equity process. The alpha engine combines three long-term compensated factor channels: price-based technical structure, sentiment-derived narrative structure, and cross-sectional peer-cluster structure. The model architecture is optimized for non-linear interactions and regime dependence, with emphasis on preserving signal monotonicity and implementation realism.

On held-out testing, the cluster-news stacked model demonstrates consistent directional uplift over baseline technical-only configurations, while the downstream multi-horizon learned meta-layer materially improves risk-adjusted returns. After applying execution-realism corrections (1-day lag, 5 bps one-way cost), the strongest gross configuration — Learned Meta Ridge — recorded Sharpe +2.85 (46-day OOS, gross of strict cost caps). Learned Meta XGB achieved Sharpe +2.19 over a 99-day OOS window. The Buy-and-Hold benchmark achieved Sharpe +6.12 and +2.61 over the same respective windows, outperforming all active strategies in absolute terms. Under strict walk-forward OOS with institutional cost scenarios (5–20 bps), all active strategies produce deeply negative net Sharpe (range −2.97 to −4.58). This report concludes with an institutional FAIL status pending resolution of the open issues documented in Section 7.

Portfolio Construction Recommendation: Deploy the signal as a medium-conviction tactical overlay (target risk budget: 8-15% of active risk) within an existing multifactor book. Use regime filters to dynamically scale gross exposure during volatility expansion and policy-sensitive macro windows.

1.1 Alpha Proposition in Multifactor Context

  • Role in stack: orthogonal enhancer to value, quality, and low-volatility premia.
  • Primary edge: non-linear capture of sentiment-momentum interactions under cluster conditioning.
  • Risk intent: incremental information ratio improvement without materially increasing beta leakage.
  • Balance sheet lens: supports tangible common equity preservation through drawdown-aware sizing.

1.2 Quantitative Risk Appetite Statement

The strategy is calibrated for moderate-to-high quantitative risk appetite in liquid US equities, conditioned on robust risk overlays. Under stressed market regimes, target behavior is drawdown containment rather than unconditional return maximization. Signal confidence should be discounted in severe liquidity fragmentation or abrupt policy re-pricing episodes.

3. Model Architecture & Formulation

3.1 Architecture Overview

The production candidate is a stacked gradient-boosting architecture with three layers: baseline technical regressor, direct/cluster sentiment augmentation, and multi-horizon meta-signal governance. XGBoost is selected over deep sequence alternatives due to superior robustness in tabular, interaction-heavy, medium-sample settings.

Table 2. Architecture Components and Functional Purpose
Layer Method Purpose Output
Signal Core XGBRegressor Capture non-linear price-feature interactions T+1 baseline forecast
Narrative Enhancer Direct + Cluster news stacking Residual correction from sentiment channels Directional uplift
Meta Allocation Learned Ridge / XGB meta-model Regime-aware horizon combination Tradeable alpha score

3.2 Formal Signal Definition

Let \(\mathbf{x}^{tech}_{i,t}\) denote technical factors, \(S^{dir}_{i,t}\) direct sentiment, and \(S^{clu}_{i,t}\) cluster-pooled sentiment. The model uses exponentially decayed sentiment memory with evidence-driven hyperparameters.

\[ w_k = \frac{e^{-\lambda(k-1)}}{\sum_{m=1}^{L} e^{-\lambda(m-1)}},\quad S^{clu}_{i,t} = \sum_{k=1}^{L} w_k \cdot \frac{1}{|P(i)|}\sum_{j \in P(i)} s_{j,t-k} \]
\[ \hat{y}_{i,t+1} = f\!\left(\mathbf{x}^{tech}_{i,t}, S^{dir}_{i,t}, S^{clu}_{i,t}, \varphi(c(i))\right) \]

3.3 Handling Fat Tails and Non-Linearity

  • Tree ensembles capture threshold effects and non-linear cross-terms without explicit specification.
  • Walk-forward validation limits overfitting to transient tail events.
  • Residual-stacked sentiment layer avoids over-dominance of noisy text factors.
  • Regime filters dampen exposures in elevated VIX and disagreement states.

This design is suitable for return distributions with excess kurtosis and asymmetric shock response, where linear factor assumptions often understate downside tail co-movement.

4. Analysis, Backtesting & Evaluation

4.1 Regression and Directional Evaluation

Evaluation combines point-error metrics (MAE, RMSE, R²) and directional utility (DHR). The cluster-news stacked implementation improved directional accuracy versus baseline while preserving high explanatory fit.

Important: Near-1.0 R² Interpretation (02D Framework). The R² values near 0.9997 do not indicate look-ahead bias or a trivial problem. The 02D model predicts the next-day closing price level (a highly autocorrelated quantity), not the one-day log-return. Near-unit R² on price levels is expected even for a naïve random-walk forecast. The financially meaningful metrics are directional accuracy (~51%) and cross-validated MAE on returns (CV_MAE ≈ 1.1%). R² on price levels must not be used as a signal-quality criterion.

4.2A Active-Return Attribution Framework (Required for Institutional Readout)

To avoid benchmark interpretation bias, strategy evaluation should be decomposed into active components:

\[ r^{active}_t = r^{strategy}_t - r^{benchmark}_t,\quad IR = \frac{\mathbb{E}[r^{active}_t]}{\sigma(r^{active}_t)}\sqrt{252} \]

Claims of alpha should be based on positive and stable active return, not only on standalone Sharpe. In this report version, this decomposition is a required next-step and should be treated as pending evidence.

5. Execution, Liquidity & Transaction Cost Analysis

5.1 Execution Framework

The alpha score is intended for liquid large-cap US equities with daily rebalance tolerance. Execution should be integrated with participation-rate limits, opening-auction concentration caps, and volatility-adjusted order slicing.

5.2 Liquidity Reallocation Logic

  • Increase weight in names with stable spread/volume profile and aligned multi-horizon signals.
  • Reduce exposure where signal strength is high but liquidity-adjusted impact estimate is elevated.
  • Reallocate gross notional from disagreement buckets to high-confidence consensus buckets.

5.3 TCA Design (Implementation Proposal)

Ex-post and simulated TCA should include: effective spread, temporary impact, adverse selection, and slippage versus decision price. A practical pre-trade approximation can be expressed as:

\[ \mathrm{Cost}_{i,t} = a \cdot \mathrm{Spread}_{i,t} + b \cdot \left(\frac{\mathrm{Size}_{i,t}}{\mathrm{ADV}_{i,t}}\right)^\gamma + c \cdot \sigma_{i,t} \]

Orders are accepted only when expected alpha net of expected costs remains positive and exceeds a minimum confidence threshold.

6.5 Model Selection Governance (Nested Protocol)

To remove selection ambiguity, strategy ranking should follow a strict nested protocol:

  1. Freeze feature definitions and data lags before model comparison.
  2. Use inner walk-forward loops for tuning and variant selection only.
  3. Use a fully untouched outer holdout for final evaluation once.
  4. Prohibit selecting the “best” strategy on the same sample used for final performance reporting.
  5. Record rejected variants and hyperparameter trials to avoid implicit cherry-picking.

7. Conclusion and Institutional Decision Gate

This project delivers a coherent ML-driven equity signal framework that combines technical structure, sentiment augmentation, and cluster-aware routing. The learned meta-layer consistently improves directional accuracy and gross risk-adjusted performance within the active strategy set.

However, the evidence base does not currently meet the threshold for institutional alpha approval. Key findings from the remediation audit:

  • All active strategies are outperformed by Buy-and-Hold in the primary OOS window.
  • All strategies produce deeply negative net Sharpe under all three institutional cost scenarios (see Table 5A).
  • The walk-forward OOS covers only 5 windows × 20 days = 100 days (not the 195 days previously claimed).
  • Nested validation has not been implemented; the outer holdout may have been exposed to implicit selection.
  • Key reproducibility artefacts (pinned environment, file hashes, git commit) are missing.
