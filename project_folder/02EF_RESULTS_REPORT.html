6. 02F — Regime-Filtered Signal Results

02F combines the T+1 short-term signal (EMA carry proxy from 02D) and the T+15 medium-term signal from 02E using three stacking mechanisms: (1) VIX-conditional weighting, (2) regime filter, (3) sentiment velocity alignment. It also trains Ridge and XGBoost meta-learners on the combination.

6.1 Key Findings

⚠ Rule-Based Meta performs poorly (Sharpe −3.72) The fixed weighting rule (w_t1=0.70, w_t15=0.30 in low-VIX) is hurt by the T+15 signal being directionally wrong at T+1 timescales — a different forecast horizon predicts a different direction. Simple blending of T+1 and T+15 raw scores is inadvisable. The meta-learner approach (below) correctly learns to avoid this trap.
✓ XGBoost Meta-Learner is highly effective (Sharpe +5.44, DHR 68.1%) The XGBoost meta-learner learns that the raw T+15 score should be used as a regime indicator, not added directly to the T+1 score. When VIX is low and T+15 is positive, it increases T+1 confidence; when T+15 disagrees, it discounts the T+1 signal. This is the correct interpretation of the multi-horizon combination.

8. VIX Regime Analysis

The test period (2017) was characterised by predominantly Low VIX (VIX < 18), with the "Volatility of 2017" maintaining near-historically-low levels before spiking in early 2018. This explains why the Low VIX bucket dominates.

ℹ Interpretation of VIX regime results In Low VIX conditions, the model applies 70% weight to T+1 and 30% to T+15. The meta-learner learns that in 2017's steady bull trend, longer-horizon momentum signals (EMA cross, momentum_12_1) are reliable — backing NVDA and GOOG which were in clear uptrends. This matches the feature importance findings.

False Breakout Suppression

A "false breakout" occurs when T+1 predicts UP but T+15 predicts DOWN. These events represent short-term bounces against the medium-term trend. The meta-signal correctly discounts a portion of these, as shown by the lower average realized return on false-breakout vs aligned days.

10. Diagnosis & Issues Found

Issue 1 — Zero Sentiment Features (Critical)

All 6 sentiment-based features have importance = 0.0. Root cause: the 02C_daily_sentiment_scores.csv file loaded by 02E contains only zeros because the FinBERT sentiment pipeline cached empty scores during the 02C run. This eliminates all information from the sentiment_velocity, sentiment_acceleration, sent_lag*, and sent_roll5 features.

✅ Fix: Re-run the FinBERT scoring step in 02C with force_refresh=True, or directly call the Kaggle news pipeline in 02E to fetch fresh scores. Alternatively, use the 02D_cluster_news_features.csv which has multi-layer sentiment (direct + cluster + macro) and was populated correctly in 02D.

Issue 2 — T+15 Signal Used Directly as T+1 Predictor in Rule-Based 02F (Severe)

The rule-based meta signal adds the T+15 log-return prediction to the T+1 carry proxy with a fixed weight. A T+15 prediction is not a prediction of T+1 direction — it predicts the cumulative log return 15 days ahead. When the market is trending up over 15 days but pulls back on day 1, adding a positive T+15 prediction increases the long signal on a day when the actual T+1 return is negative. This creates a systematic directional misalignment.

✅ Fix: In the rule-based filter, use T+15 only as a regime indicator (positive/negative medium-term trend), not as an additive score. Specifically: meta_signal = sign(T+1) × (1.0 if sign(T+15) == sign(T+1) else discount_factor). The XGB meta-learner already learned to do this implicitly, which is why it outperforms.

Issue 3 — Short Test Window (47 days)

The test window is only 47 trading days (~2 months). Sharpe ratios computed on 47 observations have very high standard errors (SE ≈ Sharpe/√47 ≈ 0.79 for Sharpe=5.44). The confidence interval on the XGB meta Sharpe includes a wide range from ~3.9 to ~6.9.

✅ Fix: Expand to at least 252+ test days by extending data to 2019–2020. Use walk-forward validation (retrain every 60 days) rather than a single train/test split.

Issue 4 — FAST_MODE Limits Generalisation

Results are based on only 5 stocks: AAPL, MSFT, GOOG, AMZN, NVDA. These are mega-cap technology stocks with correlated regimes, meaning the cluster-aware training may not add much benefit (all 5 may land in 1–2 clusters). The results may not generalise to the full S&P 500 universe.

✅ Fix: Run with FAST_MODE=False on the full universe. Expected to see more variance across cluster models and potentially better overall performance as the cluster structure becomes more meaningful with 300–500 stocks.
